The problem

A core system had run the business for twelve years — and become too risky to touch. The original team had moved on, documentation was thin, and a single bad deploy could halt operations. But it couldn’t be switched off either.

What we did

We modernised it the safe way: strangler-fig style, replacing one capability at a time behind a stable interface, with the old and new running side by side until each piece was proven. Nothing went live until it had been shadow-tested against real traffic.

  • Mapped and documented the legacy behaviour first
  • Replaced capabilities incrementally behind a façade
  • Ran old and new in parallel, comparing outputs
  • Cut over piece by piece, reversible at every step
“Our customers never knew anything changed. That was exactly the point.” — CTO (sample quote)

The result

The business now runs on a modern, maintainable system — migrated with zero outages and full data integrity. And because we built it, we keep it healthy.
